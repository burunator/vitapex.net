I Dealt With Sciatica For 6 Years — Until Someone Showed Me Why Nothing Was Working

MR
Michael R., 64 — retired, Colorado Springs
Posted 3 days ago · 629,346 views

I want to start by saying I'm not a doctor. I'm not a health writer. I'm just a 64-year-old man who spent six years waking up every morning wondering if today would be the day I couldn't get out of bed anymore.

The shooting pain started in my lower back and ran straight down my left leg. Some days it was a dull burn. Other days it felt like someone was driving a hot nail through my hip. I saw three different doctors. Did physical therapy twice. Tried the injections. Took the medications until my stomach gave out.

Every single time, the answer was the same: "It's your disc. There's not much we can do beyond managing it."

I accepted that for years. I stopped hiking. I stopped playing with my grandkids the way I used to. I started planning my days around where I could sit down.

"Then a friend — another guy dealing with the same thing — sent me a link. He said: just watch the first ten minutes."

I almost didn't click it. I'd been down that road before. Every "natural solution" and "breakthrough discovery" I'd ever tried had done exactly nothing.

But what this doctor said in the first few minutes stopped me cold. He pulled up a study from the North American Spine Society showing that nearly 90% of people over 50 have disc problems — but feel zero pain whatsoever.

Think about what that means. The thing I'd been told was the cause of my pain for six years… might have had nothing to do with it at all.

He went on to explain that there's a small structure deep inside the lower back — one that traditional imaging rarely identifies and that conventional treatments never target. And when that structure is disrupted, the sciatic nerve doesn't just flare up. It starts a slow process of deterioration. Each episode gets a little worse than the last.

This is the part I can't stop thinking about

He explained that the treatments most people rely on — the ones doctors prescribe automatically — may actually be accelerating this process without anyone realizing it. I won't say more than that here. You need to hear him explain it himself.

I watched the whole thing. Twice. And I did the small thing he recommended that same night.

I'm not going to tell you my life changed overnight. But I will tell you that three weeks later I went on a two-mile walk with my wife. First time in four years. She cried. I pretended not to.

I don't know if it'll do the same for you. But after six years of being told there was no answer — finding out that nobody had even been looking at the right place changed everything for me. And I figured if one person reads this and watches that video, it was worth writing.
